Borehole drilling

Borehole drilling involves drilling and casing a hole to access groundwater. Two broad classes of water borehole drilling are shallow or unconfined wells completed within the uppermost saturated aquifer at that location, and deep or confined wells, sunk through an impermeable stratum into an aquifer beneath.

Test pumping

Test pumping is a field experiment in which a well is pumped at a controlled rate and water-level response is measured in observation and response data from pumping tests are used to estimate the hydraulic properties of aquifers, evaluate well performance and identify aquifer boundaries it also determines the properties of a pump to be used.
